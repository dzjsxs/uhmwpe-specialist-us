Borated UHMWPE (Ultra-High Molecular Weight Polyethylene) sheet is a high-performance composite radiation shielding material produced by uniformly dispersing boron carbide (B₄C) powder into UHMWPE resin under high-temperature compression molding. The boron-10 isotope in B₄C has an exceptionally high neutron absorption cross-section (3,840 barns), making it one of the most effective thermal neutron absorbers available. Combined with the hydrogen-rich UHMWPE matrix — which moderates fast neutrons — the result is a lightweight, non-toxic, and mechanically robust neutron shielding panel suitable for demanding nuclear industry applications.

We supply borated UHMWPE sheets in standard boron carbide (B₄C) loadings of 5%, 10%, 15%, and 30% by weight. Applications: Nuclear Power Plants & Research Reactors

In nuclear power plants, borated UHMWPE is used for spent fuel pool rack liners, dry cask storage inserts, reactor cavity shielding walls, and personnel access door panels. The material's neutron absorption capability prevents criticality incidents in spent fuel storage. For research reactors and particle accelerators (cyclotrons, synchrotrons), borated UHMWPE panels are used as beam stop inserts, shielding cave walls, and neutron beam collimators. Our nuclear-grade material is manufactured under ISO 9001:2015 quality management and can be supplied with third-party test certificates from SGS, Bureau Veritas, or Intertek.

Medical Accelerator & Radiation Therapy Shielding

Medical linear accelerators (LINACs), proton therapy centers, and PET/CT facilities require effective neutron shielding in vault walls and ceiling panels. Borated UHMWPE is preferred over concrete and lead for these applications because it is lightweight (reducing structural load), non-toxic, easy to machine, and provides superior neutron attenuation per unit thickness. Our 5% and 10% borated UHMWPE sheets are widely used in LINAC vault construction, meeting NCRP Report No. 151 and IAEA Safety Standards Series requirements. We also supply lead-boron polyethylene composite panels (Pb/B₄C/PE) for combined gamma and neutron shielding.

Borated UHMWPE vs. Concrete vs. Lead Shielding

Compared to conventional shielding materials, borated UHMWPE offers significant advantages: it is 6-8× lighter than concrete (density ~1.0 vs. ~2.3 g/cm³), non-toxic unlike lead, and provides superior neutron moderation due to its high hydrogen content. Unlike lead, which only attenuates gamma radiation, borated UHMWPE simultaneously moderates fast neutrons (via hydrogen) and absorbs thermal neutrons (via boron-10). The material does not produce secondary gamma radiation after neutron capture, unlike some other shielding materials. For applications requiring both gamma and neutron shielding, we offer lead-boron polyethylene composites with lead powder loadings of 30-70% by weight.
