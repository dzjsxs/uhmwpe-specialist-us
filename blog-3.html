5 Industries Where Engineering Plastics Are Replacing Metal Components

Jiasheng Plastic · June 2025 · 5 min read

The Metal-to-Plastic Transition in Industry

Over the past two decades, engineering plastics have increasingly replaced metal components in demanding industrial applications. The driving factors include weight reduction, corrosion elimination, noise reduction, and — most importantly — dramatically lower total cost of ownership.

Here are five industries where the transition from metal to engineering plastics is delivering measurable results.

1. Mining & Bulk Material Handling

Mining operations face extreme wear conditions that destroy conventional materials. UHMWPE has emerged as the dominant material for:

  • Chute and hopper liners
  • Conveyor belt scrapers and wear strips
  • Truck body liners
  • Crusher feed chutes

Case Study: A copper mining operation in Chile replaced AR400 steel chute liners with UHMWPE wear plates. Results: 4.8× longer service life, 72% reduction in replacement labor costs, and elimination of material buildup that previously required manual clearing every 8 hours.

Mining ROI Data

Average ROI for switching from steel to UHMWPE liners: 280% over 3 years, based on data from 47 mining operations across 12 countries.

2. Food & Beverage Processing

Food processing equipment requires materials that are hygienic, easy to clean, and resistant to cleaning chemicals. UHMWPE and HDPE have replaced stainless steel in many applications:

  • Conveyor wear strips and guide rails
  • Cutting boards and work surfaces
  • Tank and vessel linings
  • Pump and valve components

Beyond hygiene benefits, plastic components reduce noise levels by 8-15 dB compared to metal, improving worker safety and comfort.

3. Marine & Offshore

Corrosion is the primary enemy of metal components in marine environments. Engineering plastics offer inherent corrosion immunity:

  • Dock fenders and bumpers (UHMWPE, HDPE)
  • Pile guides and fendering systems
  • Offshore platform walkway grating
  • Underwater wear components

A North Sea offshore platform replaced bronze bushings with UHMWPE alternatives, eliminating corrosion failures and reducing maintenance intervals from 6 months to 3+ years.

4. Automotive & Transportation

Weight reduction drives plastic adoption in automotive applications. Engineering plastics reduce component weight by 40-60% compared to metal equivalents:

  • Conveyor system components in assembly plants
  • Wear pads and guide rails
  • Truck body liners and floor protection
  • Agricultural equipment wear parts

5. Chemical & Pharmaceutical Processing

Chemical resistance and purity make engineering plastics essential in chemical processing:

  • PTFE and UHMWPE for aggressive chemical environments
  • PEEK for high-temperature, high-pressure applications
  • POM for precision valve and pump components
  • HDPE for chemical storage tanks and piping
