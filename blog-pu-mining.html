Polyurethane Sheet in Mining & Aggregate Industry

How Cast PU Elastomer Outperforms Rubber in Screening and Wear Applications

Jiasheng Plastic | Dezhou, Shandong, China | Cast Polyurethane Manufacturer
Home › Blog › Polyurethane Sheet in Mining & Aggregate Industry...

Cast polyurethane sheets have become the material of choice for mining screen panels, conveyor liners, and hydrocyclone components. With 3–5× better abrasion resistance than natural rubber and superior oil resistance, PU delivers longer service life and lower total cost of ownership in demanding mining environments.

Why Polyurethane Outperforms Rubber in Mining

Natural rubber has been used in mining for decades, but cast polyurethane offers critical advantages: 3–5× lower wear volume in DIN abrasion tests, oil and fuel resistance, higher load-bearing capacity, and significantly reduced noise levels (10–15 dB quieter than steel screens).

Key Applications: Screen Panels

Polyurethane screen panels for vibrating screens are available in modular bolt-down designs (305×305mm or 610×305mm). Standard hardness for screen panels is Shore A 70–85, balancing aperture retention with impact resistance. Open area can be customized from 20% to 55%.

Conveyor Impact Bars and Skirt Boards

At conveyor transfer points, PU impact bars (Shore A 50–65) absorb energy while protecting the belt. PU skirt board liners seal the conveyor edge to prevent spillage. Both components typically last 3–5× longer than rubber equivalents in abrasive applications.

Hydrocyclone Liners

Cast PU liners (Shore A 90–95) provide excellent wear resistance in high-velocity zones. PU hydrocyclone liners typically outlast rubber by 2–3× in fine grinding circuits, significantly reducing maintenance downtime and replacement costs.
