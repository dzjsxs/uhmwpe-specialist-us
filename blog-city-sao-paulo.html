São Paulo, Brazil's industrial and financial capital, anchors Latin America's largest manufacturing economy. The Greater São Paulo metropolitan area — with over 21 million inhabitants and a GDP exceeding USD 400 billion — hosts Brazil's most concentrated industrial clusters: automotive manufacturing (Volkswagen, Mercedes-Benz, Toyota, Honda, Stellantis), mining equipment and services (Vale, Gerdau), sugar-ethanol processing (Brazil is the world's largest sugarcane ethanol producer), food and beverage processing, and a rapidly expanding infrastructure sector. Brazil's vast mineral wealth — including the world's largest iron ore reserves in Minas Gerais and extensive bauxite, manganese, and nickel deposits — creates enormous demand for wear-resistant engineering plastics.

UHMWPE Wear Liners for Brazilian Iron Ore Mining

Brazil's iron ore mining industry — dominated by Vale's operations in the Carajás and Quadrilátero Ferrífero regions — is the world's second-largest, producing over 400 million tonnes annually. The handling of iron ore from mine to port involves extensive conveyor, chute, and ship-loading systems that are subject to severe abrasive wear. UHMWPE wear liners installed in transfer chutes, loading spouts, and ship-loader hoppers dramatically reduce maintenance intervals and extend equipment life. Our UHMWPE liners are available in standard and custom profiles, with thicknesses from 10mm to 150mm and surface textures optimised for smooth material flow in high-tonnage iron ore applications.

Polyurethane Screens for Brazilian Mineral Processing

Brazil's diverse mineral processing industry — encompassing iron ore, bauxite (Hydro, Norsk Hydro), manganese, nickel (Vale's Onça Puma), and phosphate (Mosaic, Vale) — requires extensive screening equipment for ore classification, dewatering, and product sizing. Our polyurethane screen panels are engineered for the specific characteristics of Brazilian ores, with aperture sizes from 0.5mm to 150mm and panel dimensions to suit all major screen frame systems used by Brazilian mining operators. We supply panels compatible with Metso Outotec, Sandvik, Haver & Boecker, and Schenck screen systems.

HDPE and Nylon for Brazilian Automotive Manufacturing

The ABC Paulista automotive cluster — São Bernardo do Campo, Santo André, and São Caetano do Sul — and the broader São Paulo state automotive belt produce over 2 million vehicles annually. Tier 1 and Tier 2 automotive suppliers throughout Greater São Paulo require precision-machined nylon (PA6, PA66), POM, and PEEK components for under-hood applications, transmission components, and structural parts. Our engineering plastic products are supplied with full material traceability documentation and are available in automotive-grade formulations meeting ABNT (Brazilian Standards Association) specifications.

Shipping to São Paulo: Port of Santos

We ship to São Paulo via the Port of Santos — Brazil's largest and Latin America's busiest container port, handling over 4.5 million TEUs annually — located approximately 70km from central São Paulo. Standard sea freight transit time from our factory in Hebei Province, China, to Santos is 32-40 days via the Panama Canal route. Import duties on engineering plastics to Brazil are typically 12-18% under Brazil's TEC (Common External Tariff), with additional IPI, PIS, and COFINS taxes. We work with customs brokers experienced in Brazilian import procedures.

Brazilian Standards (ABNT) and Certifications

Our products for the Brazilian market comply with relevant ABNT (Associação Brasileira de Normas Técnicas) standards and international ISO/ASTM equivalents. HDPE pipe products meet ABNT NBR 15561 specifications. For food-contact applications, products comply with ANVISA (Brazilian Health Regulatory Agency) Resolution RDC 56/2012. Third-party test certificates from SGS Brasil, Bureau Veritas Brasil, or Intertek Brasil are available upon request.
