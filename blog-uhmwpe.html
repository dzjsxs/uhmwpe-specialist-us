UHMWPE Sheet Properties: Complete Technical Reference

📅 2025 ⏱ 10 min read 🔬 Engineering Plastics

Ultra-High Molecular Weight Polyethylene (UHMWPE) sheet is one of the most versatile engineering plastics available today. With a molecular weight ranging from 3.5 to 9.2 million g/mol — far exceeding standard HDPE — UHMWPE delivers a unique combination of mechanical, chemical, and tribological properties that make it indispensable across dozens of industries. This guide covers everything engineers and procurement professionals need to know about UHMWPE sheet properties.

Molecular Structure and Weight

UHMWPE derives its exceptional properties from its extremely long polymer chains. Standard HDPE has a molecular weight of 50,000–250,000 g/mol, while UHMWPE ranges from 3.5 to 9.2 million g/mol. These ultra-long chains create an entangled network that dramatically improves impact strength, wear resistance, and chemical resistance compared to conventional polyethylene grades. Mechanical Properties

UHMWPE sheet exhibits outstanding mechanical performance: Tensile strength of 20–40 MPa, Elongation at break of 200–400%, Impact strength (notched Izod) of 80–200 kJ/m², Flexural modulus of 700–1,000 MPa, and Hardness of 60–65 Shore D. These properties make UHMWPE sheets ideal for applications requiring both toughness and flexibility, such as wear liners, chute linings, and impact-absorbing components.

Abrasion and Wear Resistance

UHMWPE has a wear resistance 15 times greater than carbon steel and 6 times greater than brass. Its coefficient of friction (0.05–0.20) is among the lowest of any solid material, comparable to PTFE. This makes UHMWPE sheet the preferred choice for conveyor wear strips, guide rails, and sliding surfaces in bulk material handling. www.jslt-conveyor.com specializes in UHMWPE conveyor components that outlast steel alternatives by 5–10x.

Chemical Resistance

UHMWPE sheet demonstrates excellent resistance to a wide range of chemicals including: concentrated acids (HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃), alkalis (NaOH, KOH), organic solvents, oils, and fuels. It is not recommended for use with oxidizing acids above 70% concentration or aromatic/chlorinated hydrocarbons at elevated temperatures. This broad chemical resistance makes UHMWPE sheet ideal for chemical processing equipment, tank liners, and laboratory applications.

Temperature Performance

UHMWPE sheet performs reliably across a wide temperature range: Service temperature: -200°C to +80°C (continuous), Short-term peak: up to +90°C, Cryogenic performance: excellent, retains toughness at liquid nitrogen temperatures (-196°C). This cryogenic capability makes UHMWPE sheet uniquely suitable for LNG storage facilities, cold chain logistics, and cryogenic equipment components.

Food Safety and FDA Compliance

UHMWPE sheet meets FDA 21 CFR 177.1520 requirements for direct food contact applications. It is also compliant with EU Regulation 10/2011 for food contact materials. This makes UHMWPE the material of choice for food processing equipment, cutting boards, conveyor components in food plants, and pharmaceutical manufacturing. Available Grades and Specifications

UHMWPE sheet is available in multiple specialized grades: Standard (natural white), UV-stabilized (outdoor applications), Antistatic (ESD-sensitive environments), Colored (identification/safety coding), Filled grades (glass fiber, carbon fiber for enhanced stiffness), and Radiation-shielding (boron-filled for nuclear applications). Standard sheet sizes range from 1,000×2,000mm to 1,220×2,440mm, with thicknesses from 3mm to 200mm. Custom sizes are available from manufacturers like www.uhmwpesheetpro.com.
