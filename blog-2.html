Engineering Plastic Selection Guide: Choosing the Right Material for Your Application

Jiasheng Plastic · June 2025 · 5 min read

How to Select the Right Engineering Plastic

Selecting the wrong plastic material can lead to premature failure, increased maintenance costs, and production downtime. This guide helps engineers and procurement professionals choose the optimal material for their specific application requirements.

Key Selection Criteria

1. Mechanical Requirements

Consider the mechanical loads your component will experience:

  • Wear/abrasion: UHMWPE, Nylon, POM
  • High impact: UHMWPE, Nylon PA6
  • High load bearing: PEEK, Nylon PA66, POM
  • Fatigue resistance: PEEK, Nylon, POM

2. Temperature Range

Operating temperature is a critical factor:

  • UHMWPE: -200°C to +80°C (excellent cryogenic performance)
  • Nylon PA6/66: -40°C to +120°C
  • HDPE: -50°C to +80°C
  • POM (Acetal): -40°C to +100°C
  • PTFE: -200°C to +260°C
  • PEEK: -60°C to +250°C

Quick Selection Matrix

Mining/bulk handling → UHMWPE | Food processing → UHMWPE, HDPE, POM | High temperature → PEEK, PTFE | Precision parts → POM, PEEK | Low friction → PTFE, UHMWPE

3. Chemical Environment

Different plastics offer varying levels of chemical resistance:

  • Excellent acid/alkali resistance: PTFE, UHMWPE, HDPE, PP
  • Good solvent resistance: PEEK, POM, Nylon (dry)
  • Food-grade compliance: UHMWPE, HDPE, POM, PTFE

Material Comparison Table

The following comparison covers the most common engineering plastics used in industrial applications. For detailed specifications, visit www.jsltupe.com.

  • UHMWPE: Best wear resistance, impact strength, chemical resistance. Ideal for liners, guides, wear plates.
  • Nylon PA6: Good mechanical properties, easy to machine. Ideal for gears, bearings, bushings.
  • HDPE: Cost-effective, food-grade, good chemical resistance. Ideal for tanks, cutting boards, marine applications.
  • PTFE: Lowest friction, highest temperature resistance. Ideal for seals, bearings, chemical equipment.
  • POM (Acetal): High precision, low friction, dimensional stability. Ideal for gears, cams, precision parts.
  • PEEK: Highest performance, extreme temperature/chemical resistance. Ideal for aerospace, medical, high-performance applications.

Application-Specific Recommendations

Mining & Quarrying

For chute liners, hopper linings, and wear plates: UHMWPE is the clear choice. Its combination of wear resistance, impact strength, and low friction coefficient reduces material buildup and extends service life 3-5× compared to steel.

Food & Beverage Processing

For cutting boards, conveyor components, and tank linings: UHMWPE or HDPE are FDA-approved options. UHMWPE offers better wear resistance for high-throughput applications.

Conveyor Systems

For chain guides, wear strips, and slider beds: UHMWPE provides the best combination of wear resistance and self-lubrication. For precision components like sprockets and rollers, Nylon or POM may be preferred.

Cost Considerations

While PEEK and PTFE offer exceptional performance, they are significantly more expensive than UHMWPE, Nylon, and HDPE. For most wear and structural applications, engineering plastics like UHMWPE and Nylon provide the best performance-to-cost ratio.
